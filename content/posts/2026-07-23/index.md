AI models are becoming more specialized every day.

Some are fine-tuned for specific industries. Some are built for agents. Some are optimized for speed, cost, reasoning, coding, or domain-specific workflows.

But bringing these models to users is still harder than it should be.

For model providers, distribution is often fragmented. It is difficult to get visibility, understand usage, define pricing, and turn demand into revenue. For developers, finding the right model still means jumping between platforms, comparing inconsistent pricing, and managing different integration paths.

**DGrid Model Marketplace is built to make this process simpler for both sides.**

## What Is DGrid Model Marketplace?

DGrid Model Marketplace is an AI infrastructure platform for publishing, discovering, and monetizing AI models — with quality verified on-chain.

It gives model providers — whether they build models from scratch, fine-tune existing ones, or run the compute to deploy them — a direct path to market, while giving developers a unified destination to discover, compare, and access models through a single API.

The platform is built around three core ideas:

- **Open model distribution** — providers list, price, and monetize their models directly
- **Unified model access** — developers discover, compare, and integrate models in one place
- **Verifiable inference quality** — powered by DGrid’s Proof of Quality (PoQ) mechanism

The Marketplace is the supply side of the DGrid network — where models come in. It works hand in hand with DGrid’s AI Gateway, the access side, where those models are called through one unified API. Together, they connect everyone who creates AI with everyone who uses it.

## Model Listing: A Direct Path to Market

Building a strong model is only part of the challenge. Bringing it to the right users, pricing it effectively, and monetizing real demand are equally critical.

DGrid Model Marketplace gives approved model providers a direct path to market. Whether you build models from scratch, fine-tune existing ones, or run the compute infrastructure to deploy them, you can list your models, set pricing, monitor usage in real time, and earn verified on-chain revenue as they’re called.

Models listed on DGrid go through a review process, helping maintain marketplace quality for developers and organizations that rely on them.

For AI builders, this means more control over how their models are distributed, accessed, and monetized.

## Unified Access: One API, All Models

For developers, model access should not feel like infrastructure fragmentation.

It should not require switching across platforms, managing separate accounts, comparing inconsistent pricing pages, or maintaining different integration paths.

DGrid Model Marketplace provides a single destination to discover, compare, and access AI models.

Through DGrid’s unified API, developers can evaluate model options, test capabilities, and integrate models into applications, agents, and enterprise workflows with less operational friction.

**One marketplace. One API. Multiple models.**

## Proof of Quality: Trust Built Into AI Inference

Access alone is not enough.

As AI models become available through more providers and distributed inference channels, users need confidence in what is being executed. DGrid addresses this through **Proof of Quality (PoQ)** — the core trust mechanism of the DGrid ecosystem.

Rather than auditing user traffic, PoQ independently and randomly tests providers against DGrid’s own benchmark set, then publishes the verification results on-chain. It never touches or exposes user request data. PoQ evaluates provider quality across multiple dimensions:

- **Accuracy alignment** — comparing outputs against reference answers or expected results
- **Response consistency** — measuring deviation across repeated executions or across nodes
- **Format compliance** — verifying adherence to required output structures

These verification results are surfaced at the model level, giving developers a more objective basis for comparison: not just provider claims, but independently verified performance.

In an open model marketplace, trust cannot be assumed. It needs to be built into the infrastructure.
